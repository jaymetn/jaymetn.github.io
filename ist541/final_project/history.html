MICHELIN Guide Asia: A Short History of Tofu



Tofu Timeline


164 B.C.E.

The most common theory explaining the origin of China states that it was invented in Northern China by Lord Liu An, a Han Dynasty prince.

965 A.D.

Tofu is first mentioned in China in the document “Ch'ing I Lu (Anecdotes, simple and exotic)” by T'ao Ku. His story describes tofu as an inexpensive food option.

1183 A.D.

Tofu is first mentioned in Japan by a Shinto priest at Nara. They used it as an altar offering.

1489

The word “tofu” is first written in Japan using the same characters today.

1665

A Westerner, Domingo Fernández de Navarrete, first mentions tofu in his book, “A Collection of Voyages and Travels.”

1770

Benjamin Franklin mentioned tofu in a letter to John Bartram in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania.

1880

Tofu is first made in Europe by Paillieux.

1878

Wo Sing and Co. became the first tofu company in the USA (San Francisco, CA).

1895

Hirata and Co. is the first Japanese-American company to start making tofu in Sacramento, CA.

1958

Tofu is first sold in Supermarkets across the United States of America.

1966

Tofu is first packaged in plastic tubs like we see today.

1997

House Foods America Corporation holds a ceremony for being the largest American tofu factory.
