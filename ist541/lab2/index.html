How to Make Tofu Miso Soup

By Jayme Nakamura


Step 1: Gather Your Ingredients

Collect the following ingredients:

  • 4 cups dashi stock
  • 1/4 cup miso paste
  • 8 oz tofu (any preferred firmness, cubed)
  • 2 tablespoons dried wakame (seaweed)
  • 2 green onions (green parts only, chopped)

You can mix instant dashi packets with water to make your stock or make it homemade. Click here to learn how to make homemade dashi stock.

(Yonemura, 2020)

Step 2: Rehydrate the Wakame

Soak the dried wakame in warm water in a small bowl for five minutes.

(Yonemura, 2020)

Step 3: Create a Miso Slurry

Mix 1/2 cup of the dashi stock with the miso paste in another bowl. Mix the two ingredients till it is of a smooth consistency.

(Yonemura, 2020)

Step 4: Add the Dashi Broth in a Pot Over the Stove

Add the remainder of the dashi stock to a pot over medium heat. Wait until it begins to simmer or bubble, then turn off the heat. Add the miso slurry and stir.


notification_important Tip: Never boil the miso once you've added it to the pot. It will result in a loss of nutrients and flavors.


(Love and Lemons, 2022)

Step 5: Add the Remaining Ingredients to the Pot

Once the stock and miso are fully incorporated, add the remaining ingredients to the pot.

(Yonemura, 2020)

Step 6: Serve in a Small Bowl and Enjoy

Pour a portion of soup into a small bowl and enjoy! If you would like, serve with a bowl of white rice.

(Yonemura, 2020)
